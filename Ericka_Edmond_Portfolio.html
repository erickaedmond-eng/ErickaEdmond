Onboarding Strategy

30 · 60 · 90 Day Plan

A structured ramp-up designed to deliver value from day one — listening first, then contributing, then leading.

30
Days — Listen & Learn
Foundation & Discovery
  • Meet with all key stakeholders — exec sponsors, tech leads, business owners
  • Audit active projects, existing documentation, and current tooling stack
  • Map team communication patterns and escalation paths
  • Understand current reporting cadence and status frameworks
  • Identify quick wins and low-hanging process gaps
  • Review existing RAID logs, project charters, and SOWs
  • Understand budget structure and procurement processes
  • Shadow sprint ceremonies, standups, and steering committee meetings
60
Days — Contribute & Refine
Ownership & Optimization
  • Take full ownership of assigned project workstreams
  • Formalize or improve RAID logging and risk escalation processes
  • Establish standardized status reporting templates for stakeholders
  • Build relationships with vendors, 3rd party teams, and dependent squads
  • Identify resource constraints and propose mitigation strategies
  • Deliver first independent stakeholder update / steering committee deck
  • Tighten sprint backlog hygiene and milestone accountability
  • Begin knowledge transfer documentation for the team
90
Days — Lead & Scale
Delivery & Impact
  • Deliver first measurable milestone or project phase on time
  • Present 90-day retrospective and forward roadmap to leadership
  • Propose process improvements backed by data from first 60 days
  • Formalize PM playbook or runbook for the team's recurring workflows
  • Establish KPIs and success metrics for all active workstreams
  • Mentor or support junior team members as needed
  • Drive cross-functional alignment meetings with autonomy
  • Begin planning next quarter's project portfolio roadmap

SAP S/4HANA × Salesforce Service Cloud Integration

Project Information
Project NameSAP-SFDC Finance Integration

Start DateDecember 2024

Success Criteria
  • 100% data integrity across ERP-to-CRM pipeline
  • 200+ users trained and live at rollout
  • Post-launch support tickets reduced vs. baseline
  • Zero data loss during migration
  • Finance + Customer Service sign-off at UAT
Business Objective

Eliminate data silos between Finance (SAP S/4HANA) and Customer Service (Salesforce Service Cloud) by establishing a real-time, bi-directional data integration. This will reduce manual reconciliation effort, improve billing accuracy, and give Customer Service teams visibility into financial data at the point of interaction.

In Scope
  • ERP-to-CRM pipeline via MuleSoft/Boomi middleware
  • Service order and billing cycle data mapping
  • UAT coordination and go-live for 200+ global users
  • Training documentation and knowledge base
  • Post-launch support transition to BAU team
Out of Scope
  • Full SAP upgrade (separate program)
  • Salesforce platform enhancements beyond integration fields
  • Invoice reconciliation module (Phase 2 consideration)
